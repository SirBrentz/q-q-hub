Understanding Exam Types

Quidget supports two consultation types from Qualiphy: GFE (General Fitness Examination) and Rx (Prescription). Each has different workflows, outcomes, and patient experience.

GFE Consultations (rx_type = 1)

GFE consultations are for non-prescription services where the provider issues clearance or approval without a prescription:

  • Examples: Botox clearance, filler approval, laser clearance, wellness assessments
  • Provider Outcome: Approval or deferral (no prescription issued)
  • Patient Receives: Clearance to proceed with the service from an aesthetics provider
  • Order Status After Approval: Order marked complete—no additional fulfillment steps
  • Pharmacy Involved: No—GFE consultations don't route through pharmacies

Rx Consultations (rx_type = 2)

Rx consultations involve prescription medications that are filled and shipped by a partner pharmacy:

  • Examples: GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy), testosterone therapy, HRT, other prescriptions
  • Provider Outcome: Prescription issued (or rejection if patient doesn't qualify)
  • Patient Receives: Prescription that's automatically routed to a partner pharmacy for fulfillment
  • Pharmacy Routing: After approval, prescription is routed to the correct pharmacy based on patient state and consultation type
  • Tracking: Patient receives pharmacy notifications, shipping tracking, and can monitor delivery from WooCommerce order
  • Order Status After Shipment: Order marked complete once pharmacy ships the medication

Cart Limits for Rx

Only one Rx consultation product can be in the cart at a time. If a customer adds an Rx product when another Rx product is already in their cart, the previous Rx product is removed. This prevents complex prescriptions and billing issues.

Checking the Exam Type

You can see the exam type (GFE or Rx) in:

  • Qualiphy clinic dashboard under Consultations list
  • WooCommerce product page (when viewing consultation product)
  • Order detail page under the Qualiphy section

GFE vs Rx Consultations

Here's a detailed comparison of how GFE and Rx consultations flow through Quidget:

Aspect GFE Rx
Examples Botox, fillers, laser clearance GLP-1, testosterone, HRT
Checkout Flow Patient info → Payment Patient info → Dose selection → Payment
Exam Invite Sent immediately after payment Sent immediately after payment
Provider Reviews Exam questionnaire, approves/defers Exam + dose history, approves/defers
After Approval Order marked Complete, patient approved Prescription created → Routed to pharmacy
Fulfillment No fulfillment (clearance only) Pharmacy fulfills, ships medication
Pharmacy Involved No Yes (required)
Tracking N/A Shipment tracking synced to order
Cart Limit Multiple GFE products allowed Only 1 Rx product per order

Key Workflow Differences

GFE Workflow

  1. Patient purchases consultation → Exam invite sent
  2. Patient completes exam → Provider reviews
  3. Provider approves → Order marked Complete
  4. Patient receives clearance notification

Rx Workflow

  1. Patient purchases consultation (selects dose) → Exam invite sent
  2. Patient completes exam → Provider reviews
  3. Provider approves → Prescription created
  4. Prescription routed to pharmacy → Pharmacy fulfills
  5. Pharmacy ships medication → Tracking updated to order
  6. Order marked Complete when shipped

Syncing Consultations

Consultations are synced from your Qualiphy clinic account to your WordPress site. Syncing fetches the latest consultation list, types, pricing, and pharmacy data.

Manual Sync

  1. Go to Qualiphy → Consultations in your WordPress admin
  2. Click the Refresh Consultations button at the top
  3. Quidget fetches the latest consultations from the Qualiphy API (/exam_list endpoint)
  4. New consultations are added, updated consultations are refreshed
  5. You'll see a confirmation message with the number of consultations synced

Sync Cooldown

There's a 5-minute cooldown between manual syncs. This prevents excessive API calls and ensures data stability. You can sync again after 5 minutes have passed.

Auto-Deactivation

During a sync, any consultations that are no longer in the Qualiphy API response are automatically deactivated. This keeps your list clean if you archive or delete consultations in Qualiphy.

Hidden Consultations

These consultations are filtered out during sync and don't appear in Quidget (even though they exist in Qualiphy):

  • Test Exam (internal test consultation)
  • PBM Example Pharmacy (example pharmacy data)

Auto Sync on Webhook

In addition to manual syncs, consultations are automatically refreshed when Qualiphy sends webhook updates about exam status, prescriptions, or pharmacy changes.

Pharmacy Data Syncing

When you sync consultations, pharmacy routing data is also synced. This includes which pharmacies serve which states for each consultation type. See Pharmacy & Fulfillment for details on configuring pharmacy routing.

Using the Import Wizard

The Import Wizard converts Qualiphy consultations into WooCommerce products. Use this wizard to create patient-facing products with pricing, variants (doses), and fulfillment settings.

Accessing the Wizard

  1. Go to Qualiphy → Consultations
  2. Click on a consultation in the list
  3. Click Import to WooCommerce button
  4. The wizard opens with that consultation pre-selected

Step 1: Select Consultation

Choose which Qualiphy consultation to import. Only one consultation per product. If you need multiple consultations as one product, you'll need to create it manually.

Step 2: Categories

Select WooCommerce product categories for this consultation (e.g., "Telehealth", "Dermatology", "Weight Loss"). This helps organize products in your store.

Step 3: Product Type

Choose between creating a new product or linking to an existing WooCommerce product. Most of the time you'll create a new product.

Step 4: Free Checkout Option

Decide if this consultation is free or paid. Free consultations skip payment and go directly to exam delivery. Paid consultations require payment before the exam is sent.

Step 5: Pharmacy Selection

For Rx consultations only. Choose how pharmacies are assigned:

  • Manual Selection: Customer selects their preferred pharmacy at checkout
  • Auto-Route: Quidget automatically selects the pharmacy based on the patient's state

Step 6: Selection Type

For dose-based consultations (GLP-1, HRT, etc.), choose how doses are presented:

  • Standard: Customer selects their dose/strength at checkout
  • Provider Select: Provider chooses the dose after reviewing the exam (patient doesn't select)

Step 7: Variants

Create WooCommerce product variants for each available dose/strength. For each variant, configure:

  • Dose/Strength Name: Display name (e.g., "0.5mg", "2mg")
  • SKU: Stock keeping unit (e.g., "glp1-05mg", "glp1-2mg")
  • Qualiphy Dose ID: Links this variant to the Qualiphy consultation dose

Step 8: Pricing

Set prices for each variant and configure per-product margins:

  • Price: Customer-facing price
  • Cost: What Qualiphy/pharmacy charges (used to calculate margin)
  • Return Fee: Fee charged to patient if exam is rejected/deferred
  • Sign-up Fee: One-time fee if this is a subscription product

Step 9: Summary

Review all settings before creating the product. Go back to edit anything before confirming.

Step 10: Create or Link

Create the new WooCommerce product with all configured settings, or link to an existing product.

Step 11: Success

Product created! It's now available in your WooCommerce store and shows as a Quidget consultation product. Customers can purchase it immediately.

The Consultation Lifecycle

Here's the complete flow from purchase to completion for a patient consultation:

Phase 1: Purchase & Checkout

  1. Customer adds product to cart: Patient selects consultation type and optional dose
  2. Checkout: Quidget collects patient data:
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth (age validation enforced)
    • Phone Number
    • Email address
    • State (checked against service area)
    • Custom fields (if configured)
  3. Payment: Customer completes payment (or skipped if free consultation)
  4. Order Created: WooCommerce order is created, marked as pending

Phase 2: Exam Invite

  1. Webhook Triggered: Payment confirmation triggers the exam invite process
  2. API Call to Qualiphy: Quidget calls the Qualiphy API with patient data:
    • patient_exam_id (unique ID for this patient's exam)
    • exam_id (consultation ID)
    • Patient demographics (name, email, DOB, phone)
    • Dose info (if applicable)
  3. Exam Invite Email: Patient receives email with link to start exam (branded with clinic colors and logo)
  4. Popup or Redirect: If auto-launch is enabled, exam modal opens on thank-you page showing exam details and meeting link

Phase 3: Patient Completes Exam

  1. Patient clicks exam link: Taken to Qualiphy exam platform
  2. Questionnaire: Patient fills out medical history, exam questions, dose preference (for Rx)
  3. Exam Submission: Exam marked as "submitted" in the system
  4. Patient notified: Patient sees confirmation and meeting link for provider consultation

Phase 4: Provider Review

  1. Provider accesses exam: In Qualiphy clinic dashboard, provider reviews patient exam and questionnaire
  2. Video Consultation (optional): Provider conducts video consultation with patient (meeting link scheduled)
  3. Provider Decision: Provider approves, defers, or rejects the exam

Phase 5: Exam Approved (GFE)

  1. Webhook Update: Qualiphy sends "exam_approved" webhook to Quidget
  2. Order Updated: WooCommerce order status set to Completed
  3. Order Notes: "Exam approved by Dr. [Name]" note added to order
  4. Patient Notified: Approval notification email sent to patient
  5. Fulfillment Complete: No additional steps—GFE consultations end here

Phase 5: Exam Approved (Rx)

  1. Webhook Update: Qualiphy sends "exam_approved" webhook with prescription details
  2. Order Updated: WooCommerce order status set to Processing
  3. Prescription Created: Prescription details extracted and stored in order
  4. Pharmacy Routing: Prescription routed to correct pharmacy based on patient state and consultation type
  5. Pharmacy Notified: Pharmacy receives prescription and begins fulfillment
  6. Patient Notified: Approval notification email sent with pharmacy contact info

Phase 6: Pharmacy Fulfillment (Rx Only)

  1. Pharmacy Ships: Pharmacy fulfills prescription and ships medication to patient
  2. Tracking Update: Qualiphy sends webhook with tracking number
  3. Order Updated: Quidget adds tracking number to order:
    • If WooCommerce Shipment Tracking plugin installed: Adds tracking to plugin
    • If WooCommerce 10.2+: Uses WooCommerce Order Fulfillment feature
    • Otherwise: Adds tracking as order note
  4. Order Status: Marked as Completed once shipped
  5. Patient Receives: Medication delivery + tracking info in email and order page

Phase 7: Exam Rejected or Deferred

  1. Webhook Update: Qualiphy sends "exam_rejected" or "exam_deferred" webhook
  2. Order Status: Set to Cancelled or Failed
  3. Auto Refund: If enabled, payment automatically refunded to original payment method
  4. Patient Notified: Rejection notification email sent
  5. Order Notes: Rejection reason added to order history
  6. Patient Options: Can resubmit, contact support, or request refund

Resending Exam Invites

If a patient didn't receive their exam invite or the link expired, you can resend it manually from the order page.

How to Resend

  1. Go to WooCommerce → Orders
  2. Click on the patient's order
  3. In the Qualiphy Consultation section, find the action dropdown
  4. Select "Send Exam Invite" or "Resend Consultation Link Email"
  5. Click submit or confirm
  6. Email is resent to the patient immediately

When to Resend

  • Patient didn't receive the initial invite
  • Patient lost the invite link
  • Exam invite email bounced or was marked as spam
  • Patient needs a reminder to complete their exam

What Gets Sent

The resent email is identical to the original invite, containing:

  • Clinic branding (logo, accent colors)
  • Patient's name and consultation type
  • Link to start the exam
  • Clinic contact info

Frequency Limit

You can resend exam invites as many times as needed—there's no limit. However, avoid excessive resending to prevent patient confusion.

The Dose Questionnaire

The dose questionnaire is a 3-step guided form that helps returning patients select their preferred medication dose and adjustment preference. It appears during checkout for consultations that support variable dosing.

When It Appears

The questionnaire is shown for:

  • Rx consultations with dose/strength variations (GLP-1, HRT, testosterone, etc.)
  • Only when enabled in product settings (enabled by default)
  • If the consultation type supports dose selection

The Three Steps

Step 1: Medication Experience

Question: "Have you taken this medication before?"

Options:

  • Yes
  • No
  • Not Sure

This helps the provider assess whether the patient is experienced with the medication.

Step 2: Current Dose

Question: "What is your current dose?"

Options: Dropdown of available dose/strength variants (e.g., 0.5mg, 1mg, 2mg, 3mg, etc.)

Patient selects their current dose or "Not taking it yet" if new to the medication.

Step 3: Dose Adjustment Preference

Question: "What is your dose adjustment preference?"

Options: Three colored buttons representing:

  • Stay: Keep current dose the same (shown with purple bottle icon)
  • Increase: Increase the dose (shown with green bottle icon)
  • Decrease: Decrease the dose (shown with orange bottle icon)

Patient indicates their preference. The provider will consider this when determining the approved dose.

Customization

All questionnaire text is customizable in Qualiphy → Settings → Language:

  • Step titles and question text
  • Dose option labels (Stay, Increase, Decrease)
  • Button colors and bottle icons
  • Disclaimer text

Disabling the Questionnaire

To disable the questionnaire:

  1. Go to Qualiphy → Settings → Products
  2. Uncheck "Enable Questionnaire by Default"
  3. Or disable per-product in the product edit page

Flow After Questionnaire

  1. Patient completes questionnaire
  2. Responses saved to order
  3. If "Questionnaire Redirect to Checkout" is enabled, proceeds directly to checkout
  4. If disabled, shows review/summary page before checkout
  5. Patient completes payment
  6. Exam invite sent with questionnaire data included (provider sees patient's dose preference)

Provider Access

When the provider reviews the exam in Qualiphy, they see the patient's questionnaire responses including:

  • Whether they've taken the medication before
  • Current/previous dose
  • Requested dose adjustment (stay, increase, or decrease)

The provider uses this information to determine the appropriate approved dose.
